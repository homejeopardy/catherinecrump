About

Catherine Crump is a Clinical Professor of Law and Director of the Samuelson Law, Technology & Public Policy Clinic at UC Berkeley School of Law. Her work focuses on the intersection of law, technology, and civil liberties, with an emphasis on surveillance, government accountability, and the impact of emerging technologies on democratic institutions.

Before joining Berkeley Law, she served as a staff attorney at the American Civil Liberties Union, where she litigated high-profile cases involving freedom of speech, digital rights, and privacy.
