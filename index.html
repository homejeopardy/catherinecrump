Professor of Law at UC Berkeley

Catherine Crump is a Clinical Professor of Law and Director of the Samuelson Law, Technology & Public Policy Clinic at UC Berkeley School of Law. Her work focuses on the intersection of law, technology, and civil liberties.

About

Professor Crump teaches and litigates in areas related to technology, privacy, and government accountability. She is a nationally recognized expert on surveillance, freedom of expression, and the impact of emerging technologies on democratic institutions.

Before joining Berkeley Law, she was a staff attorney at the American Civil Liberties Union, where she litigated high-profile cases involving free speech and digital rights.

Research

  • Surveillance and its impact on marginalized communities
  • Transparency and accountability in government use of technology
  • Privacy rights in the digital age
  • The intersection of law, democracy, and emerging technologies

Teaching

Professor Crump teaches courses and supervises student work in the Samuelson Law, Technology & Public Policy Clinic.

  • Law & Technology Policy
  • Privacy and Civil Liberties in the Digital Age
  • First Amendment and Emerging Technology
