About

Catherine Crump is a clinical professor and Director of the Samuelson Law, Technology & Public Policy Clinic at UC Berkeley School of Law. She is an expert on legal issues related to police surveillance technology and has litigated constitutional challenges in federal courts to protect First and Fourth Amendment rights. Her work also involves compelling the disclosure of government records under the Freedom of Information Act.

Prior to her work at Berkeley Law, she was a staff attorney at the American Civil Liberties Union (ACLU) for nearly nine years, where she litigated cases on electronic surveillance and political protest rights. She also served as a Senior Policy Advisor for Criminal Justice to the White House Domestic Policy Council. She holds a J.D. from Stanford Law School and a B.A. from Stanford University.

In the Media

Professor Crump is a frequent commentator and opinion writer in national news outlets, including The Washington Post and CNN. She has testified before Congress and the European Parliament.

TED Talk

The small and surprisingly dangerous detail the police track about you

In her popular TEDGlobal 2014 talk, Catherine Crump reveals how a mundane-sounding technology—the automatic license plate reader—is used to create massive databases that track the movements of ordinary citizens, raising serious civil liberties concerns.
